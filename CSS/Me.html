Hey I'm Me.

I’m me — not by accident, not by design, but by a thousand tiny choices, moments, and thoughts stitched together.
I’m the quiet mornings I spend with my own mind, the loud laughter that bursts out at the wrong time, the doubts that keep me up, and the dreams that keep me going.
I’m me because I’ve stumbled, stood up, and sometimes sat down to catch my breath. I’m me because I’ve cared too much and sometimes too little.
I’m me because I’ve lived in the past, worried about the future, and tried, every day, to be present in the now.

Why?

  • Because being true to yourself brings inner peace.
  • Because no one else can live your life or think your thoughts.
  • Because authenticity builds real connections with others.
  • Because you grow most when you embrace who you already are.

Why not?

  • Because sometimes who you are now might hold you back from who you could become.
  • Because being “just me” can turn into an excuse to avoid change or effort.
  • Because people might misunderstand or judge you for showing your real self.
  • Because blindly clinging to “I’m me” could mean ignoring flaws that need work.

I’m not perfect, not finished, and never final. And that’s the best part — I’m me, becoming.

You should you

You should be you — wildly, stubbornly, unapologetically.
You should be the person who laughs at odd things, who cries at small kindnesses, who loves what you love even if no one else understands why.
You should be the voice that disagrees when it matters, the heart that stays soft when the world says “harden.”
You should keep your quirks, your questions, your quiet moments of wonder — because those are yours alone.
You should be you, even when it feels easier to blend in, to shrink, to silence your spark.
Because someone, somewhere, needs to see that it’s okay to simply exist as yourself — incomplete, imperfect, but real.
You should be you — because there is no better person you could possibly be.

Why?

  • Because it’s freeing to live without pretending.
  • Because your uniqueness might inspire someone else to do the same.
  • Because life feels lighter when you’re not wearing a mask.
  • Because only you can bring your full, authentic perspective into the world.

Why not?

  • Because sometimes adapting a little makes relationships and teamwork smoother.
  • Because parts of who you are might still be harmful to yourself or others and need reflection.
  • Because stubbornly insisting on “just being you” can close you off from growth.
  • Because in certain moments, compromise or restraint might be wiser.

We should be both

We should be both — you and me, separate yet side by side.
We should be the comfort in each other’s silence and the spark in each other’s laughter.
We should be strong when the other feels weak, gentle when the other feels raw. We should hold space for each other’s shadows and dance together in each other’s light.
We should be both — individuals with our own dreams and fears, yet also something more when we come together: a shared story, a quiet understanding, an unspoken promise.
We should keep our edges, keep our differences, but let them fit together like pieces of the same strange, beautiful puzzle.
Because being both means we don’t lose ourselves — we find each other.

Why?

  • Because together, we can share strengths and soften weaknesses.
  • Because connection adds meaning that solitude alone can’t give.
  • Because being both individuals and a “we” creates balance: freedom with belonging.
  • Because love or friendship deepens when we respect each other’s individuality.

Why not?

  • Because merging too much can blur personal boundaries.
  • Because relying on “we” might make it harder to stand alone when needed.
  • Because conflict can arise if the “both” starts to feel unequal.
  • Because sometimes individual paths demand space, even from those we love.
